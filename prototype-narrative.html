Hello, World.

I am Alexander, a designer obsessed with simplicity and clarity.

For the past ten years, I have lived my life at the intersection of pixel-perfect typography and robust software engineering. My philosophy is surprisingly simple: technology should get out of the way. If a user notices the interface, I haven't done my job right.

Currently, I spend my days in Cupertino, California. At Apple, I lead the core design systems architecture for macOS. It means I spend hours obsessing over the exact curvature of a window shadow or the precise spring tension of a scroll animation. Before that, I shaped narrative digital experiences as a Creative Director at Vogue Labs in New York, where we prioritized huge, impactful imagery over dense blocks of text.

But I am more than the products I help ship. When I close my laptop, you'll usually find me wandering the fog of San Francisco with a medium-format film camera, studying mid-century modern furniture, or reading about behavioral economics. I believe that being a good designer requires borrowing ideas from fields entirely unrelated to design.

What I can do for you

I shape brand identities.

I don't just draw logos. I build visual languages from the ground up, ensuring that every touchpoint—from the app icon to the tone of voice in a push notification—feels uniquely yours.

I build complex interfaces.

With deep expertise in React and modern CSS architectures, I design in the browser. I bridge the gap between Figma mockups and production code, ensuring the final product matches the vision entirely.

Let’s have a conversation.

I am always open to discussing new ideas, architecture revamps, or simply grabbing coffee in the Bay Area to talk about typography.
